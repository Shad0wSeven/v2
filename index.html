hi! i'm ayush nayak, and since 2004 i've been finding fascinating problems. currently I'm at mit, working on a few different things, and going on way too many trips.

i live life to collect stories: turning a hackathon into a rave, dumpster diving a car, living out of a lifeguard tower for two nights, potentially taking a one way sign in broad daylight... i'm trying to balance creating the most interesting stories while doing the most interesting work.

things i'm interested in:
everything about llm's, especially multi-llm networks, surfing, film photography, drones, cinematography, astronomy, skateboarding, design, crypto, silicon, e-guitar, hardware, 3d art, and stealing signs.

in the past i designed and built bleeding-edge 3-valued transistors independently to discovering transit-timing-variable exoplanets, and launching a chip-sat to the moon. I've worked for nasa, ucsd, northrop, and many others, dabbling in incredibly robust system design, to building for research.
